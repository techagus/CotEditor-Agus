If the Yen sign appears as a backslash in CotEditor

With one of the text encodings listed below, a halfwidth Yen character (¥) is displayed as is when you type, but if you change the encoding, or, if you save and reopen the file, it will be displayed as a backslash (\).

  • Japanese (Windows, DOS)
  • Japanese (EUC)
  • Traditional Chinese (EUC)
  • Simplified Chinese (GB 2312)
  • Korean (EUC)
  • Korean (Windows, DOS)
  • Thai (Windows, DOS)
  • Thai (ISO 8859-11)
  • Arabic (Mac OS)
  • Arabic (ISO 8859-6)
  • Hebrew (Mac OS)
  • Greek (ISO 8859-7)
  • Cyrillic (Mac OS)
  • Cyrillic (ISO 8859-5)
  • Cyrillic (Windows)
  • Central European (ISO Latin 2)
  • Central European (ISO Latin 4)
  • Central European (Windows Latin 2)
  • Central European (Mac OS)
  • Nordic (ISO Latin 6)
  • Baltic (ISO Latin 7)
  • Celtic (ISO Latin 8)
  • Romanian (ISO Latin 10)
  • Russian (DOS)
  • Latin-US (DOS)
  • Western (ISO Latin 3)
  • Western (ASCII)
